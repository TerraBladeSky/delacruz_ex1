Roguelike References

Below is a list of resources used in building this informational site on roguelike games. These include official game sites, reviews, community wikis, and historical references that provide insights into both classic and modern roguelikes, with special focus on our top recommended titles.

Top Recommended Games (Official Sites)

  • Dead Cells by Motion Twin ★★★★★ (5/5) Official site for our #1 recommended roguelike. Includes game information, updates, DLC details, and community resources for this masterclass in roguelite design that blends Metroidvania-style combat with procedural generation.
  • Risk of Rain 2 by Hopoo Games ★★★★★ (5/5) Official resource for our #2 ranked game. Features information on survivors, items, monsters, and updates for this thrilling 3D roguelike shooter with its brilliant risk-reward system.
  • Hades by Supergiant Games ★★★★★ (5/5) Official page for our #3 pick. Contains information on this narrative-driven roguelike masterpiece that redefined storytelling in the genre through its Greek mythology setting.
  • BlazBlue Entropy Effect ★★★★☆ (4/5) Official site for our #4 ranked game that successfully combines fighting game mechanics with roguelike procedural generation and progression.
  • Balatro ★★★★☆ (4.5/5) Official page for our #5 recommendation. Features details on this innovative poker-inspired roguelike deck builder that transforms familiar card game concepts into a strategic roguelike experience.

Historical Roguelike Resources

  • Wikipedia – Roguelike Comprehensive overview of the roguelike genre, its history from Rogue (1980) through modern titles, and defining characteristics according to the Berlin Interpretation.
  • RogueBasin Community wiki dedicated to roguelike development and history, including documentation on traditional roguelikes and the evolution of the genre.
  • Game Developer – What is a Traditional Roguelike? Detailed analysis of the Berlin Interpretation and how it defines traditional roguelike games versus modern roguelites.

Classic Roguelike Official Sites

  • Official NetHack Site Home of one of the most influential traditional roguelike games from 1987, featuring documentation on its complex systems and interactions.
  • Ancient Domains of Mystery (ADOM) Website for ADOM, featuring updates, guides, and community resources for this classic roguelike that introduced character development and multiple endings.
  • Dwarf Fortress Official site for this incredibly deep roguelike simulation that continues to influence the genre and push the boundaries of complexity.

Other Influential Modern Roguelikes

  • Spelunky World Official site for the pioneering platformer roguelite that helped popularize procedural generation and permadeath in action games.
  • The Binding of Isaac Edmund McMillen's influential twin-stick shooter roguelite that helped bring the genre to mainstream attention in 2011.
  • Enter the Gungeon Fast-paced bullet hell roguelite that combines precise combat with procedurally generated dungeons.

Critical Analysis and Reviews

  • PC Gamer – Roguelike vs Roguelite Analysis of the differences between traditional roguelikes and modern roguelite games, exploring how the genre has evolved.
  • Rock Paper Shotgun – Best Roguelike Games Curated list of top roguelike games with detailed reviews and recommendations that informed our own rankings.
  • Eurogamer – Dead Cells Review In-depth analysis of Dead Cells' combat mechanics and progression systems that make it our top recommendation.
  • IGN – Hades Review Critical examination of how Hades successfully blends narrative with roguelike mechanics, earning it the #3 spot in our recommendations.
